# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

University students and course participants (18–35) meeting their lecturer before or during a course. They open this link on a phone or laptop, likely in a few spare minutes. Their job: calibrate how much to trust and invest in this person. Secondary audience: professional contacts, conference organisers, collaborators who land here via LinkedIn.

## Product Purpose

Course introduction page for Dr. Stefan Bauer — PhD, CEO Sustainista, EdTech innovator, university lecturer across 10+ institutions. The page exists to answer one question before students ask it: "Who is this person and why should I care?" Success looks like: student arrives at first lecture already sold, LinkedIn follow rate visibly growing, professionals reaching out for collaboration.

## Brand Personality

Credible · Bold · Human

Voice: direct, energetic, no fluff. Tone: confident without posturing, warm without oversharing. This is the professor who publishes in top journals AND hosts a 200-person hackathon AND cares about 14-year-old apprentices learning to save money. That range is the brand.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate-Grau**: bland, interchangeable, no point of view. Like a university HR page or a Word-exported CV.
- **SaaS-Startup-Cliché**: gradient text on hero, big metrics in glassmorphism cards, "built for teams" vibes. The exact aesthetic that makes someone look like a vendor, not a person.
- **Akademisch-steif**: serif fonts, wall-of-text bios, the kind of restraint that reads as insecurity. Research-paper energy on a personal portfolio.
- **Overloaded-Agency**: scroll-jacking, ten animations firing at once, visual noise that competes with content. Impressive for 8 seconds, exhausting after that.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over adjective** — Never say "leading expert." Show the €17,880 research prize, the 620+ citations, the award photo with the cheque. The credentials carry the claim.
2. **Editorial clarity** — Structure like a Wired feature. Large image, spare white-space, typography that creates pace and hierarchy. The reader's eye should always know where to go next.
3. **Human at scale** — The page is impressive but not intimidating. Stefan is someone you want to learn from, not someone you're afraid of. Every section should feel like a person wrote it.
4. **One section, one job** — Each scroll stop does exactly one thing. The award section says: this person won something enormous. The Skills-UP section says: this is why it matters. Don't make sections compete.
5. **The follow is earned, not asked** — The LinkedIn CTA works because the page already delivered something. Place it last, after the reader has been convinced. It should feel like a natural next step, not a popup.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Readable on mobile (students often open on phone). No autoplay video or audio. Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Alt text on all images.
