6 Learnings from ~€3M managed

🔍
Define "Done" Before You Start
In L2BGreen and PACE-DPP, each deliverable had a formal acceptance criterion. Without that, every output becomes a negotiation. Write your definition of done on day one.
🤝
Trust is Built Before it's Needed
Seven partner teams across Europe, never in the same room. Investing in relationship-building in month 1 paid dividends in crisis months 18 and 24.
🚦
Status Reports Nobody Reads Are Noise
I switched from weekly text updates to a simple RAG dashboard. Response rate doubled. Short, visual, actionable beats long, text-heavy, informational every time.
🔧
Change Requests Aren't Failure
Formally requesting a change to scope or timeline is a professional process — not a sign the project is failing. What kills projects is managers who hide drift instead of surfacing it.
🧪
Prototype Early, Validate with Users
In Skills-UP!, we tested with real apprentices before scaling. Assumptions about what users need are almost always wrong until tested. Build-measure-learn works in EU projects too.
🤖
AI Cuts Coordination Overhead by ~30%
Status summaries, stakeholder drafts, risk pattern recognition — AI handles these now. The bottleneck shifts to decision-making. That's exactly where human judgment belongs.
